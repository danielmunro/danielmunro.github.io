Writing is hard, like really hard. And that is why I decided I need to start practicing again. After all, some of the best skills in life are hard to improve, yet incredibly gratifying.

And the only way to get better at something is to practice it, right?

Of Clouds And Developers

After spending about a dozen years building infrastructure and applications in AWS, I’ve had to recently take a hiatus. Not a hiatus from building and developing, but from AWS. I got a new job back in September, and let’s say things have been a bit different.

The company I work for is building out a multi-cloud strategy, with the primary pillar being Azure. I have no experience with Azure. Let’s just say, the last few months have been a huge learning experience.

Let’s be honest for a moment. Humbling myself, starting over, and learning a totally new cloud platform – really wasn’t part of my plan at this point in my career.

Discomfort Precedes Growth

While it’s been uncomfortable at times learning all the terminologies and idiosyncrasies of a new platform, the growth has been really welcome.

Let’s hope writing brings the same kind of gratification.
